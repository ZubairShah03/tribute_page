Dr. Allama Muhammad Iqbal

Philosopher, Poet, and Politician

Here's a time line of Allama Iqbal's life:

Early Life:

  • Birth: November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, British India (now Pakistan).

Education:

  • Early Education: Received his early education in Sialkot.
  • College: Attended Government College Lahore and obtained his Bachelor's degree in 1897 and Master's degree in 1899.

Higher Education:

  • 1905: Traveled to England for higher studies.
  • Degree in Philosophy: Enrolled at Trinity College, Cambridge and obtained a degree in Philosophy.
  • Doctorate: Earned a PhD from the University of Munich, Germany in 1908, with a thesis on Persian metaphysics.

Career:

  • 1908: Returned to India and became a professor at Government College Lahore.

Law:

  • 1908: Also began practicing law in Lahore.

Political Involvement:

  • 1926: Elected to the Punjab Legislative Council.
  • 1930: Delivered the famous Allahabad Address, outlining his vision for an independent Muslim state in northwestern India.

Literary Contributions:

  • Poetry: Wrote extensively in Persian and Urdu.
  • Books in Persian:
    • Asrar-e-Khudi (1915) – Secrets of the Self
    • Rumuz-i-Bekhudi (1917) – The Secrets of Selflessness
  • Books in Urdu:
    • Bang-i-Dra (1924) – The Call of the Marching Bell
    • Bal-i-Jibril (1935) – Gabriel's Wing
    • Zarb-i-Kalim (1936) – The Rod of Moses

Death:

  • April 21, 1938: Passed away in Lahore, Pakistan. Buried near the Badshahi Mosque.
